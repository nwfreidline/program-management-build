## 4. Escalation Paths

### Escalation Matrix

| Level | Escalation Point | Criteria | Response Time |
|-------|-----------------|----------|---------------|
| 1 | Project Manager | Issue cannot be resolved by team member | [24 hours] |
| 2 | Project Sponsor | Issue exceeds PM authority or budget/schedule threshold | [48 hours] |
| 3 | Steering Committee | Issue impacts multiple projects, strategic decisions needed | [1 week / next meeting] |
| 4 | Executive Leadership | Crisis, program-level impact | [Immediate] |

### Escalation Triggers
- Schedule variance > [X days/weeks]
- Budget variance > [$Amount or X%]
- Critical resource unavailable > [X days]
- Scope change impacting baseline
- Unresolved stakeholder conflict
- Critical risk materializing

### Escalation Process
1. Identify the issue and confirm it cannot be resolved at current level
2. Document the issue, impact, and recommended action
3. Notify the appropriate escalation point within the defined response time
4. Provide supporting information and options
5. Follow through on the decision and communicate resolution
